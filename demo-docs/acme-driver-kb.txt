ACME FREIGHT — DRIVER OPERATIONS KNOWLEDGE BASE (COMPILED)

Note: This knowledge base was assembled from several internal sources — the
driver handbook, dispatch memos, IT help tickets, and safety bulletins. Because
it was merged from multiple places, some guidance is repeated in more than one
section. It is intended as a single reference for over-the-road drivers.


SECTION 1 — GETTING STARTED

Welcome to Acme Freight. This section covers the basics you need in your first
week. Your driver ID is printed on the back of your badge and is required for
most support requests.

To reset your fuel card PIN, call the Fuel Services desk at extension 4400 and
verify your driver ID. A new PIN is texted to your phone on file within about
fifteen minutes. Do not share your PIN with anyone, including dispatch.

Uniforms are ordered through the company store. New drivers receive two sets of
shirts and one jacket; replacements are at the driver's expense after the first
ninety days.


SECTION 2 — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: I forgot my fuel card PIN. What do I do?
A: Call Fuel Services at extension 4400, verify your driver ID, and you will
receive a new PIN by text message in roughly fifteen minutes. PIN resets are not
handled by dispatch.

Q: How do I request paid time off?
A: PTO requests must be submitted in the driver portal at least fourteen days
before the requested dates. Requests inside fourteen days are approved only at
the terminal manager's discretion.

Q: Where do I send lumper receipts?
A: Photograph the lumper receipt and upload it in the portal under the load
number within twenty-four hours. Paper receipts mailed to the terminal are no
longer accepted.


SECTION 3 — COMMON ISSUES AND HOW TO RESOLVE THEM

Fuel card PIN resets are handled by the Fuel Services desk at extension 4400.
Have your driver ID ready. The replacement PIN arrives by SMS in approximately
fifteen minutes. This is the fastest path; opening an IT ticket for a PIN reset
will only be redirected to Fuel Services and will take longer.

If your electronic logging device (ELD) freezes, power-cycle the unit by holding
the side button for ten seconds. The current approved ELD firmware is version
4.8.2; do not let the unit update beyond 4.8.2 without IT approval, as later
builds have a known sync defect with our dispatch system.

Breakdown protocol: pull off safely, place triangles, then call the Breakdown
line before calling anyone else so the load clock can be paused.


SECTION 4 — NIGHT AND AFTER-HOURS OPERATIONS

Night operations run on a reduced staff, so expect longer hold times on routine
lines between 10 PM and 5 AM. Routine questions should wait until day shift when
possible. For genuine emergencies after hours — accidents, cargo security
incidents, or anything involving driver safety — the after-hours emergency
dispatch line is 1-800-555-0147, staffed twenty-four hours. Use it only for
true emergencies; routine fuel card or PIN issues should still go to Fuel
Services during business hours.

Detention pay begins after two hours of wait time at a shipper or receiver and
accrues at twenty dollars per hour. You must log arrival and departure in the
portal for detention to be calculated; undocumented wait time is not paid.


SECTION 5 — DISPATCH MEMO 2026-02 (REPRINTED)

Reminder to all drivers: fuel card PIN problems go to Fuel Services at extension
4400, not to dispatch. You must verify your driver ID. A new PIN is texted to
you in about fifteen minutes. Dispatch will redirect these calls, which slows
everyone down during peak hours.

Also in this memo: reroutes involving hazardous materials loads that add more
than five thousand dollars in cost require sign-off from the Director of Safety
before the truck moves. Standard non-hazmat reroutes can be approved by the
on-duty dispatcher.


SECTION 6 — ONBOARDING CHECKLIST (FOR REFERENCE)

New drivers should complete the following in week one: badge photo, portal
login, ELD pairing, and a test fuel transaction. If your fuel card PIN does not
work during the test transaction, contact Fuel Services at extension 4400 with
your driver ID and you will get a texted reset in about fifteen minutes. Confirm
your uniform order and submit any first-period PTO requests through the portal
at least fourteen days ahead.
